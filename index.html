BACKGROUND CONNECTIONS

Background

After Japan surrendered ending WWII, the Korean Peninsula was occupied by the United States in the south, and the Soviet Union in the north. The north adopted the Soviet Union’s Stalinist government system. On June 25, 1950, North Korean forces crossed the 38th parallel into South Korea, officially starting the Korean War. After 3 years of brutal conflict, a ceasefire was signed on July 27, 1953, ending the fighting between the two sides. Ever since the war, The North Korean government has been using propaganda, trying to make its citizens view countries such as the United States, Japan, and South Korea as evil. One way they do this is by portraying the U.S. as dangerous imperialists, and the North Koreans as great liberators fighting against tyranny. Due to this mentality, whenever North Korea comes in contact with an American in their country, they imprison them and torture them, labeling them as spies and informants looking to destroy the country. North Korea also labels people who try to escape the country as traitors of the regime, and torture them as well, if they get caught.

RIGHTS VIOLATIONS

Human Rights Violations

Currently, North Korea and its government currently violates 25/30 human rights outlined by the United Nations “Universal Declaration Of Human Rights”. One main human right that is violated is the one of being tortured, which is commonly violated by North Korea. People who try to escape the country and those who enter the country and are accused of something by the government are routinely tortured in North Korean forced labor camps. One example of this happening would be when Otto Warmbier was captured by the North Koreans when he attempted to take a propaganda poster with him back in 2017. He was labeled a spy and tortured to the point where he ended up dying in a coma.

Solutions

To be able to stop North Korea from torturing people, you have to go after the regime itself, and attack their oppressive ways of dealing with prisoners. One idea is to expose North Koreans to outside information that their government keeps from them. With this information they obtained, they would lose trust in their government, creating a loss of control. Another way to weaken the country would be going after their nuclear weapons. You could start a trade war with China, and since most of China’s economic output goes to the United States, this would give us leverage against them, providing a way to get them to cut of supplies to North Korea. Once they have their nuclear weapons taken away, they become less of a global threat, giving other countries more leverage to use against them. This could happen if the United States negotiates with China to be able to slow the amount of supplies going to the country, making the country more dependent on China. These solutions could end up with the people losing trust in their government, leading to an overthrow which could eventually end up with the destruction of the regime, and their power to detain and torture prisoners.
